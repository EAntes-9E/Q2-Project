Sinigang

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly (or pork ribs, shrimp, or fish, depending on your preference)
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish (labanos), peeled and sliced thinly
  • 1 cup taro (gabi), peeled and cubed
  • 1 bunch of water spinach (kangkong) or substitute with spinach
  • 10–15 pieces of string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 eggplants, sliced diagonally
  • 1 green chili (siling pangsigang), optional
  • 1 pack of sinigang mix (commonly tamarind-based)
  • 1 cup fresh tamarind (boiled and mashed to extract juice)
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice or lemon juice (alternative)
  • 1/4 cup green mango or kamias (optional)
  • 6–8 cups water

Instructions

  1. Cook the meat/seafood: In a pot, sauté onion, tomatoes, and ginger until fragrant. Add the pork, shrimp, or fish and sauté for a few minutes. For pork, you may need to simmer it until tender.
  2. Add tamarind: If using fresh tamarind, cook it with water to extract the sourness. Add tamarind paste or powder directly if using a shortcut version.
  3. Simmer: Add water and bring it to a boil. Let it simmer until the meat is cooked through and the flavors are melded together.
  4. Add vegetables: Add the vegetables and cook until they’re tender but not mushy.
  5. Season: Season with fish sauce, salt, pepper, and chili (if desired). Taste and adjust the sourness with more tamarind if necessary.
  6. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice on the side.
